# Amendment A-000 — Foundational Governance (Courts, Veto, Quorum, Signing Order)

**Amendment ID:** A-000
**Status:** Ratified
**Applies To:** NexusCanon Constitution v1.0 — Article IX (Amendment and Governance)
**Type:** Additive / Procedural Only (No new doctrine)
**Non-Retroactivity:** This amendment does not reinterpret or modify existing constitutional clauses.

> **Court Succession Notice:** Court roles SHALL be defined **by office** in **Amendment A-001**.

---

## A-000.1 Definitions (Procedural)

- **Clause:** A numbered SHALL/SHALL NOT/MAY rule in the Constitution.
- **Amendment:** A formally numbered additive text that extends the Constitution without rewriting existing clauses.
- **Interpretation Note:** A non-binding clarification published by a Court; not law.
- **Veto:** A binding rejection that blocks a proposal from ratification.
- **Quorum:** Minimum required signatories for ratification.

---

## A-000.2 The Courts (Interpretive Authority)

The system recognizes three Courts with distinct authority.

### Court I — The Doctrine Court
- **Authority:** Determines whether a proposal introduces philosophy not present in the **LBOS Origin Paper (Sealed)**.
- **Standard:** Must pass Traceability Check to an Origin term/doctrine.
- **Power:** **Hard veto** on alien doctrine.

### Court II — The Integrity Court
- **Authority:** Determines whether a proposal violates core architectural integrity (Atomic Truth, no partial reality, hard boundaries).
- **Standard:** Must pass the **Expanded Conformance Test Suite**.
- **Power:** **Hard veto** on any conformance failure.

### Court III — The Execution Court
- **Authority:** Determines whether a proposal belongs in Constitution vs Product (AXIS) vs Execution (Titan).
- **Standard:** Enforces Scope of Prohibition (no tech stacks, perf targets, UI frameworks in Constitution).
- **Power:** **Soft veto** (reclassification) unless the proposer refuses relocation.

---

## A-000.3 Veto Powers (Non-Negotiable)

A proposal SHALL NOT be ratified if any of the following occur:

1) **Traceability Failure Veto**
- The proposal cannot cite an Origin doctrine/term.

2) **Non-Retroactivity Veto**
- The proposal edits, replaces, or rewords existing constitutional clause text.

3) **Conformance Failure Veto**
- Any clause-level conformance test evaluates to **NO**.

4) **Scope Contamination Veto**
- The proposal introduces prohibited items into constitutional text (technology/vendor/performance/UI framework).

---

## A-000.4 Public Ledger (Immutable Transparency)

All amendment activity SHALL be recorded in a **Public Ledger** that is:

- **Immutable** (append-only; no deletion or modification)
- **Publicly accessible** to all stakeholders governed by this Constitution
- **Timestamped** for every entry

Minimum ledger entries SHALL include:
- Proposal submission
- Court verdicts
- Ratification vote outcome
- Publication confirmation

---

## A-000.5 Amendment Lifecycle (Required Steps)

Every Amendment SHALL follow these steps in order:

1) **Proposal Submission**
- Must include: Amendment ID draft, Target Clauses, Proposed Addition, Traceability Check, Impact Analysis (AXIS/Titan).

2) **Court Review (Order is Mandatory)**
- Doctrine Court → Integrity Court → Execution Court.

3) **Public Ledger Entry**
- Proposal and Court verdicts logged per A-000.4.

4) **Ratification Vote**
- Signing order enforced (A-000.7).
- Quorum enforced (A-000.8).

5) **Publication**
- Amendment appended as a new numbered section.
- Existing clause text remains untouched.

---

## A-000.6 Interpretation Notes (Non-Binding Governance)

Courts MAY publish Interpretation Notes for clarity, but:

- Notes SHALL be **timestamped**.
- Notes SHALL be **attributed to a specific Court**.
- Notes SHALL NOT override clause text.
- Notes SHALL NOT be used to bypass conformance tests.

If an Interpretation Note materially changes understanding, it MUST be elevated to a formal Amendment.

---

## A-000.7 Signing Order (Authority Chain)

Amendments SHALL be signed in this order:

1) **System Architect (Office)**
2) **Doctrine Court Representative (Office)**
3) **Integrity Court Representative (Office)**
4) **Execution Court Representative (Office)**
5) **AXIS Product Steward (Office)** *(required only if AXIS impact exists)*
6) **Titan Execution Steward (Office)** *(required only if Titan impact exists)*

If any required signer refuses, the amendment fails.

---

## A-000.8 Quorum Thresholds

- **Procedural Amendments** (governance-only): Minimum **3 signatures**
  - System Architect + Doctrine Court + Integrity Court

- **Doctrinal Clarification Amendments** (interpretive additions): Minimum **4 signatures**
  - + Execution Court

- **Architectural Constraint Amendments** (affect integrity boundaries): Minimum **4 signatures**
  - System Architect + Doctrine Court + Integrity Court + Execution Court

- **Cross-Layer Amendments** (affect AXIS/Titan explicitly): Minimum **5 signatures**
  - + impacted layer steward

---

## A-000.9 Emergency Path (Severe Defect Only)

If an existential defect threatens:
- Atomic Truth
- Audit integrity
- Hard boundaries

Then an **Emergency Amendment** may be ratified with:
- System Architect + Integrity Court + Doctrine Court

**Safeguard:** Emergency Amendments SHALL be **procedural only** and SHALL NOT introduce new doctrine.

A full Court Review MUST occur within **7 calendar days** or the Emergency Amendment is void.

---

## A-000.10 Ratification Record and Derivation Hashing

Every ratified amendment SHALL record:
- Amendment ID
- Signatories
- Date/time
- Short reason for acceptance

### Derivation Hash (Ongoing Integrity Proof)

- A **Derivation Hash SHALL be recomputed after each ratified Amendment**.
- The hash inputs SHALL include:
  1) Full text of **LBOS Origin Paper (Sealed)**
  2) Full text of **NexusCanon Constitution v1.0**
  3) Full text of the newly ratified **Amendment**
- The resulting hash SHALL be stored in the Public Ledger.
