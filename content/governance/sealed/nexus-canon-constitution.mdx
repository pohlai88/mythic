# NexusCanon Constitution

## Preamble

This constitution establishes the governance framework for NexusCanon-AXIS, deriving authority from the LBOS Origin Paper.

## Article I - Foundation

### Section 1: Core Values

The system shall operate according to the principles established in the LBOS Origin Paper (L0).

### Section 2: Judgment Supremacy

Human judgment remains supreme. AI systems provide recommendations but do not make binding decisions.

## Article II - Decision Making

### Section 1: Atomic Truth

Each unit of truth shall be discrete and independently verifiable.

### Section 2: Decision Memory

All significant decisions shall be recorded with:
- Decision ID
- Timestamp
- Reasoning
- Stakeholders
- Outcome

## Article III - Document Layers

### Section 1: Layer 0 (Foundational)

Immutable philosophical principles. Amendment requires supermajority approval.

### Section 2: Layer 1 (Constitutional)

Governance rules. Amendment requires majority approval.

### Section 3: Layer 2 (Operational)

Active planning. May be modified through normal procedures.

## Article IV - High-Integrity Modulith

### Section 1: Architecture

The system shall maintain a single codebase with strict module boundaries.

### Section 2: Boundaries

Module boundaries shall be enforced through:
- Clear interfaces
- Dependency rules
- Compilation checks

## Article V - Amendment

### Section 1: Process

Amendments must:
1. Be proposed in writing
2. Include rationale
3. Undergo review period
4. Receive required approval

### Section 2: Tracking

All amendments shall be tracked in the public ledger.
